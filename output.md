Q1. [Medium] According to the text, what is the primary nature of the Christian life?
A. A period of rest and peace
B. A journey of personal discovery
C. A spiritual battleground
D. A time for learning and contemplation
Answer: C

Q2. [Medium] The text states that believers do not fight _for_ victory, but rather fight _from_ victory. What does this imply about the source of their ability to overcome?
A. Victory is achieved through human effort and determination.
B. Victory is already secured by Christ's work on our behalf.
C. Victory is a future hope that motivates present struggles.
D. Victory depends on the strength of our willpower.
Answer: B

Q3. [Medium] The Apostle John's statement, 'For whatsoever is born of God overcometh the world,' suggests that overcoming is:
A. A potential outcome for diligent believers
B. A result of consistent prayer
C. An inherent characteristic of believers
D. A reward earned through good deeds
Answer: C

Q4. [Medium] Which of the following is identified in the text as a source of strength for overcoming challenges in the Christian life?
A. Personal willpower and determination
B. The wisdom gained from worldly knowledge
C. The indwelling Spirit of God
D. The promises of material wealth
Answer: C
